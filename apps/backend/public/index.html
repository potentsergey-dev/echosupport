Support gets expensive when every simple question becomes a ticket.

Most teams do not lose customers because they lack answers. They lose them because the answers live in too many places and the visitor waits while the team reconstructs context.

Repeated questions

Refunds, pricing, onboarding, and setup questions consume operator time every day.

Scattered knowledge

Docs, policies, and help pages exist, but visitors still cannot find the right answer.

Lost context

When a human joins, they often start by asking the visitor to repeat the story.

No feedback loop

Teams answer chats but struggle to see satisfaction, gaps, and follow-up demand.

EchoSupport connects the first answer to the human workflow.

The widget is only the doorway. Behind it is a support workspace for knowledge, operator handoff, CSAT, and booking-ready conversations.

Website widget

Embed the chat on any site with a public agent key and a small script.

Knowledge answers

Index documents and URLs so the assistant can answer from your material.

Operator inbox

Escalate conversations with the transcript and visitor context intact.

CSAT and booking

Close the loop with ratings, service flows, specialists, and appointments.

Built for teams that want control, not another black box.

EchoSupport is designed as a practical open-source support layer: Docker Compose for the first deployment, PostgreSQL for durable data, Qdrant for retrieval, and an admin panel your team can operate directly.
